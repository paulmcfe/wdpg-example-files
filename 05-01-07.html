David Foster Wallace

A tribute to the gifted novelist, short story writer, and essayist who died far too young

The American novelist, short-story writer, and essayist David Foster Wallace was born on February 21, 1962 in Ithaca, New York. His father was a philosophy professor and his mother was an English teacher. After growing up in Illinois (where he became a nationally-ranked junior tennis player), Wallace attended Amherst College in Massachusetts. He majored in both philosophy and English, and wrote the novel The Broom of the System as his senior English thesis.
